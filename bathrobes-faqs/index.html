Bathrobes questions, answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The best bathrobe for you depends on your climate, bathing habits, and preferred feel, terry cloth is highly absorbent and classic, while plush robes are softer and warmer. Focus on material, GSM (weight), length, and care instructions when choosing.

What is the difference between terry cloth and plush robes?

Terry cloth robes are woven with uncut loops that create a highly absorbent, textured surface, think of a thick hotel towel turned into a robe. They are excellent for drying off quickly after a shower and tend to be more durable. However, some people find them a bit stiff or heavy until broken in. Plush robes, often made from velour or microfibre fabrics like Sherpa or fleece, have a cut, brushed surface that feels incredibly soft and cozy. They are less about absorption and more about lounging warmth, ideal for slipping on after a bath to stay toasty. Plush robes are typically lighter on the skin but may not wick moisture as efficiently as terry cloth.

How heavy or light should my bathrobe be?

Bathrobe weight is measured in GSM (grams per square metre). Lightweight robes (under 300 GSM) are airy and quick-drying, perfect for warm climates or steamy bathrooms. Mid-weight robes (300–400 GSM) offer a balance of absorbency and comfort for year-round use. Heavyweight robes (400–550 GSM and above) feel luxurious and bathhouse-grade, they hold a lot of water and warmth, but they take longer to dry and can be bulky. For daily use, most people prefer a mid-weight terry cloth robe; for pure pampering, a heavyweight plush or terry robe is hard to beat.

What length bathrobe should I buy?

Robe lengths generally fall into three categories: knee-length (hits just above or at the knee), mid-calf, and full/ankle length. Knee-length robes are most popular because they allow easy movement and are less likely to drag on wet floors. They work well for most body types and daily use. Mid-calf and full-length robes provide more coverage and warmth, making them a favourite for lounging on cold mornings or after a bath. Taller individuals should look for brands that offer tall sizing or explicitly list garment length measurements, a robe that is too short can feel restrictive, while one that is too long can be a tripping hazard.

How do I care for a terry cloth or plush robe?

Terry cloth robes need gentle care to maintain their loops and absorbency. Machine wash in warm water with a mild detergent, never use fabric softener, as it coats the fibres and reduces absorbency. Tumble dry on low or medium heat, and avoid overdrying; shaking the robe out halfway through helps fluff the loops. Plush robes (velour, fleece, Sherpa) are often more delicate. Wash in cold water on a gentle cycle, and line dry or use the lowest heat setting. High heat can cause pilling or melting of synthetic fibres. Always check the care label, some luxury plush robes require dry cleaning. Regular brushing with a fabric comb can keep the nap looking fresh.

What features matter most in a bathrobe?

Pockets are a top priority for many: deep, well-placed pockets let you carry your phone, TV remote, or lip balm without worry. A secure waist tie, either a sewn-in belt or a D-ring style, keeps the robe closed comfortably. Hoods add warmth for post shower, though some people prefer a hoodless robe for a sleeker look. Reinforced seams, particularly at the shoulders and pocket edges, improve longevity. If you plan to use the robe daily or share it with a partner, look for designs that are machine washable and colourfast. A shawl collar (the classic wide lapel) adds a traditional spa feel, while a kimono-style wrap offers a more minimalist silhouette.

Should I buy a unisex robe or one designed for men/women?

Unisex robes are cut straighter through the hips and shoulders, making them a versatile choice if you prefer a looser fit or plan to share the robe. They typically come in fewer colour options and may not accommodate very curvy or very tall body shapes as well. Robe designs marketed to women often have a more tailored shape, shorter lengths, and softer colours; men’s robes tend to be longer, wider in the shoulders, and darker. The best approach is to ignore gendered labels and focus on your own body measurements, chest, waist, height, and compare them with the robe’s size chart. Many brands now offer inclusive sizing that works for a range of bodies.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best material for a bathrobe after a shower?

Terry cloth is the top choice for maximum absorbency, it wicks water away from your skin and dries you quickly. If you prefer softer fabrics, a plush robe with a cotton velour outer and looped inner can be a comfortable compromise.

Can I wear a plush robe in the summer?

Lightweight plush robes made from bamboo, rayon, or thin microfibre blends can be comfortable in air-conditioned environments, but traditional thick plush robes are better suited for cooler months. For hot weather, a thin cotton or linen robe is more breathable.

How do I know if a robe is machine washable?

Always check the care tag beforehand. Most cotton terry cloth and cotton-polyester blend robes are machine washable. Some luxury plush robes with delicate linings or decorative trims require hand washing or dry cleaning. When in doubt, cold gentle cycle and line drying is the safest bet.

Is a heavier robe always better quality?

Not necessarily. Higher GSM generally means denser fabric, which can feel more luxurious, but quality also depends on thread count, fibre length, and construction. A well-made 350 GSM robe can outlast a poorly made 500 GSM one. Look for double-stitched seams and even, dense loops in terry cloth.

What length robe do tall people need?

Tall individuals (over six feet) should look for brands that offer a ‘tall’ size or provide detailed garment length measurements. A mid-calf robe for a shorter person becomes above-the-knee on a tall frame. Aim for robes with at least a few inches of extra length beyond your height in the size chart.

Do I need a hood on my bathrobe?

A hood is great if you step out of a warm bath into a chilly room, it helps dry your hair and keeps your neck warm. However, hoods can make the robe heavier and less comfortable to wear while sitting back. Many people prefer hoodless robes for lounging and hooded ones for post-shower use.

In shortYour ideal bathrobe strikes a balance between absorbency, warmth, and feel, there is no single right answer. Start by deciding if you want a drying robe (terry cloth) or a lounging robe (plush), then pick a weight and length that fits your lifestyle and body. Care for it properly, and a well-chosen robe can become a daily comfort for years.
